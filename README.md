## The Thing That Actually Makes This Different

Most crypto cards are basically just debit cards in disguise — you sell your crypto, load it up, spend it. Done. Capital gains event triggered, ETH gone.

ether.fi Cash has two spending modes that change this entirely:

**Borrow Mode** — You deposit ETH, eETH, weETH, or stablecoins into a self-custodial vault (a Gnosis Safe you control). The protocol borrows USDC against your collateral and loads your card. Your ETH keeps earning staking yield the whole time. You spend borrowed USDC. No sale, no taxable event (in most jurisdictions — check with your accountant).

**Direct Pay Mode** — Simpler. You put USDC or other stablecoins in the vault and spend directly from that balance. Still earns APY on idle funds (~5%+ on Liquid USD). Feels like a debit card.

Both modes use the same physical or virtual Visa card. The difference is who's fronting the money.

The non-custodial angle is real, not marketing. Your assets sit in a smart contract *you* own. ether.fi doesn't hold your keys.
